MAYMAR WELFARE SOCIETY

Founded by individuals from diverse professional backgrounds, Maymar Welfare Society (MWS) was established with a singular vision: to combat poverty and illiteracy through dedicated, hands-on initiatives. MWS recognized the urgent need to address the critical gaps in healthcare and education for underserved communities, particularly in the slum areas of Karachi, and accepted these challenges wholeheartedly. Starting with limited resources and funds, MWS has tirelessly worked to improve access to medical care and educational opportunities for those who need it most. Over the years, our organization has made meaningful strides in supporting people across three key areas: Medical Care MWS has been instrumental in delivering essential healthcare to vulnerable communities by organizing free medical camps. Notably, our eye camps have reached numerous slum areas of Karachi, providing quality care to those without access. Additionally, MWS actively participates in immunization programs, including vital vaccination drives such as polio vaccinations, to protect children’s health. Education In partnership with the Sindh Education Foundation, MWS is addressing the educational needs of out-of-school children through non-formal education programs. This initiative empowers young learners, bringing them closer to mainstream education and future opportunities. Vocational Training MWS also works to uplift women by providing vocational training in collaboration with the Shaheed Benazir Bhutto Youth Development Program (SBBYDP). Through courses in dress stitching and cosmetology, a significant number of girls and women have acquired valuable skills, enabling them to earn an income and support their families. Maymar Welfare Society continues its journey of service, addressing complex social issues with compassion, dedication, and a resolve to bring lasting change in the lives of Karachi’s underserved community's
